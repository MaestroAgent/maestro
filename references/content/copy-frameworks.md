# Copy Frameworks Reference

## Core Persuasion Frameworks

### AIDA (Attention → Interest → Desire → Action)
- **Attention**: Headline or hook that stops the scroll
- **Interest**: Bridge to "this is relevant to me"
- **Desire**: Benefits, outcomes, social proof
- **Action**: Clear, specific CTA

### PAS (Problem → Agitate → Solution)
- **Problem**: Name the pain clearly
- **Agitate**: Make the pain vivid — what happens if they don't solve it?
- **Solution**: Present your offer as the answer

### BAB (Before → After → Bridge)
- **Before**: Current painful state
- **After**: Desired outcome
- **Bridge**: How your product/service gets them there

### 4 Ps (Promise → Picture → Proof → Push)
- **Promise**: Bold claim or headline
- **Picture**: Paint the outcome vividly
- **Proof**: Evidence (testimonials, data, case studies)
- **Push**: Urgency or scarcity to act now

### QUEST (Qualify → Understand → Educate → Stimulate → Transition)
- **Qualify**: "This is for you if..."
- **Understand**: Show empathy for their situation
- **Educate**: Teach something valuable
- **Stimulate**: Build desire for the solution
- **Transition**: Move to CTA

## Headline Formulas

### Number-Based
- [Number] Ways to [Achieve Desired Outcome]
- [Number] [Things] Every [Audience] Should Know About [Topic]
- [Number] Mistakes That Are [Costing/Killing] Your [Metric]

### How-To
- How to [Achieve Result] Without [Pain Point]
- How to [Achieve Result] in [Timeframe]
- How [Specific Person] [Achieved Result] (And How You Can Too)

### Question-Based
- Are You Making These [Number] [Topic] Mistakes?
- What Would You Do If [Desirable Scenario]?
- Why Do [Audience] Struggle With [Problem]?

### Curiosity Gap
- The [Adjective] Truth About [Topic]
- What [Authority] Won't Tell You About [Topic]
- I [Did Thing]. Here's What Happened.

### Direct Benefit
- Get [Result] in [Timeframe] — Guaranteed
- The Only [Tool/Method] You Need to [Achieve Result]
- Stop [Pain]. Start [Benefit]. Here's How.

## CTA Formulas

### Action-Oriented
- Start [doing desired thing] → "Start saving 10 hours/week"
- Get [specific deliverable] → "Get your free audit"
- Join [number] [people] who [benefit] → "Join 5,000 founders who..."

### Low-Friction
- "See how it works" (softer than "Buy now")
- "Try free for 14 days" (risk reversal)
- "Get the template" (specific deliverable)

### Urgency
- "Claim your spot" (scarcity)
- "Start before [deadline]" (time pressure)
- "Limited to [number] per month" (exclusivity)

## Voice Calibration

### Awareness Levels (Eugene Schwartz)
| Level | They Know | Approach |
|-------|----------|---------|
| Unaware | Nothing about the problem | Lead with story or shock |
| Problem-Aware | They have a problem, don't know solutions | Agitate the problem, introduce your category |
| Solution-Aware | Solutions exist, don't know your product | Differentiate, show why you're better |
| Product-Aware | Know your product, haven't bought | Handle objections, offer proof |
| Most Aware | Ready to buy | Clear offer, strong CTA, urgency |

### Tone Spectrum
- **Corporate**: Professional, third-person, formal
- **Professional**: Clear, first-person plural ("we"), accessible
- **Conversational**: First-person, contractions, casual
- **Provocative**: Bold claims, challenges assumptions, edgy

## Long-Form Structure

### Blog Post / Article
1. Hook (1-2 paragraphs)
2. Context / Why this matters (1-2 paragraphs)
3. Main content (organized by subheadings)
4. Summary / Key takeaways
5. CTA

### Landing Page
1. Hero: Headline + subhead + CTA + hero image
2. Problem: Articulate the pain
3. Solution: How your product solves it
4. Features/Benefits: 3-5 key points
5. Social Proof: Testimonials, logos, numbers
6. How It Works: 3-step process
7. Pricing (if applicable)
8. FAQ: Handle objections
9. Final CTA: Repeat the offer

### Sales Email
1. Subject line (under 50 chars, creates curiosity)
2. Opening hook (personal, specific, not "Hope you're well")
3. Value statement (what's in it for them)
4. Proof point (quick evidence)
5. CTA (one specific ask)
6. P.S. (secondary hook or urgency)

## Power Words by Category

### Trust
proven, guaranteed, certified, research-backed, tested, endorsed

finally, imagine, discover, transform, breakthrough, unlock, revealed

### Specificity
exact, step-by-step, complete, comprehensive, detailed, precise
