# CRO Experiment Library

## High-Impact Experiments (Proven Winners)

### Headlines & Value Propositions

#### Experiment: Benefit-First vs Feature-First Headline
- **Hypothesis:** Changing the headline from feature-focused to benefit-focused will increase signups because visitors care about outcomes, not capabilities.
- **Control:** "Advanced Project Management Software"
- **Variant:** "Finish Projects 2x Faster — Without the Chaos"
- **Metric:** Signup conversion rate
- **Typical lift:** 10-30%
- **Confidence level:** High — extensively validated across industries

#### Experiment: Specificity in Value Proposition
- **Hypothesis:** Adding specific numbers to the value proposition will increase trust and conversions.
- **Control:** "Save time with our automation tools"
- **Variant:** "Save 12 hours per week with automated workflows (based on 500+ customer average)"
- **Metric:** CTA click rate
- **Typical lift:** 15-25%

#### Experiment: Customer-Centric vs Product-Centric Language
- **Hypothesis:** Using "you/your" instead of "we/our" will increase engagement because it focuses on the visitor.
- **Control:** "We built the most powerful CRM on the market"
- **Variant:** "Your sales team closes 30% more deals with the right CRM"
- **Metric:** Scroll depth + CTA clicks

### CTAs (Call-to-Action)

#### Experiment: Action-Oriented vs Generic CTA
- **Hypothesis:** Specific, benefit-oriented CTAs convert better than generic ones.
- **Control:** "Sign Up" / "Get Started"
- **Variant:** "Start My Free Trial" / "See My Dashboard" / "Get My Report"
- **Metric:** Button click rate
- **Typical lift:** 10-40%

#### Experiment: CTA Button Color Contrast
- **Hypothesis:** Higher contrast CTA button will draw more attention and clicks.
- **Control:** Button matches page color scheme
- **Variant:** Button in contrasting color (stands out from page)
- **Metric:** CTA click rate
- **Note:** Color doesn't matter as much as contrast. The button should be the most visually prominent element.

#### Experiment: Single vs Multiple CTAs
- **Hypothesis:** Reducing to one primary CTA per page section will reduce choice paralysis and increase conversions.
- **Control:** Multiple different CTAs on the page
- **Variant:** One consistent CTA repeated at key scroll points
- **Metric:** Overall conversion rate

### Social Proof

#### Experiment: Add Customer Logos Above the Fold
- **Hypothesis:** Showing recognizable customer logos immediately builds trust and increases engagement.
- **Placement:** Below the headline, above the fold
- **Format:** "Trusted by [logos]" or "Join 5,000+ companies including..."
- **Typical lift:** 5-15% on signup pages

#### Experiment: Specific Testimonials vs Generic
- **Hypothesis:** Testimonials with specific results outperform vague praise.
- **Control:** "Great product, highly recommend!" — John D.
- **Variant:** "We increased our email open rates from 18% to 34% in 60 days" — John Doe, CEO, Company
- **Typical lift:** 10-20%

#### Experiment: Real-Time Social Proof
- **Hypothesis:** Showing recent signups/purchases creates urgency and trust.
- **Format:** "Sarah from Austin signed up 3 minutes ago"
- **Caution:** Must be real data. Fake social proof destroys trust.
- **Typical lift:** 5-15%

### Forms & Friction

#### Experiment: Reduce Form Fields
- **Hypothesis:** Removing non-essential form fields will increase completion rate.
- **Control:** Name + Email + Company + Phone + Role
- **Variant:** Email only (or Email + First Name)
- **Metric:** Form completion rate
- **Typical lift:** 10-25% per field removed
- **Note:** Each additional field reduces conversion ~10%

#### Experiment: Multi-Step vs Single-Step Form
- **Hypothesis:** Breaking a long form into steps with a progress bar will increase completion.
- **Control:** Single page with all fields
- **Variant:** 3-step form (Step 1: Email → Step 2: Details → Step 3: Preferences)
- **Metric:** Overall form completion rate
- **Typical lift:** 15-30% (commitment + consistency bias)

#### Experiment: Remove Credit Card Requirement for Trial
- **Hypothesis:** Removing credit card requirement for free trial will dramatically increase signups.
- **Trade-off:** More signups but potentially lower quality. Measure activated users, not just signups.
- **Typical lift:** 50-100% more signups, but ~40% fewer convert to paid

### Pricing Pages

#### Experiment: Highlight Recommended Plan
- **Hypothesis:** Visually emphasizing one plan will increase selection of that plan.
- **Control:** All plans equal visual weight
- **Variant:** Middle plan highlighted with "Most Popular" badge, larger card, contrasting color
- **Typical lift:** 10-25% increase in recommended plan selection

#### Experiment: Annual vs Monthly Toggle Default
- **Hypothesis:** Defaulting to annual pricing (with savings shown) will increase annual plan selection.
- **Control:** Monthly selected by default
- **Variant:** Annual selected by default with "Save 20%" badge
- **Metric:** Annual plan selection rate

#### Experiment: Decoy Pricing
- **Hypothesis:** Adding a deliberately less attractive option makes the target plan look better.
- **Example:** Basic $29 | Pro $79 | Enterprise $89 → Pro looks like best value vs Enterprise
- **Metric:** Pro plan selection rate

### Page Layout

#### Experiment: Above-the-Fold CTA Visibility
- **Hypothesis:** Ensuring the primary CTA is visible without scrolling will increase conversions.
- **Control:** CTA below the fold (after feature descriptions)
- **Variant:** CTA in hero section, repeated after each major section
- **Metric:** CTA click rate + conversion rate

#### Experiment: Remove Navigation on Landing Pages
- **Hypothesis:** Removing navigation links on dedicated landing pages reduces distractions and increases conversions.
- **Control:** Full site navigation
- **Variant:** Logo only (no nav links), or logo + single CTA in nav
- **Typical lift:** 10-30% on paid traffic landing pages

## Medium-Impact Experiments

### Trust & Anxiety Reduction

#### Experiment: Add Security Badges Near Forms
- **Hypothesis:** Trust badges near form submission reduce anxiety about data security.
- **Badges:** SSL certificate, SOC 2, GDPR compliant, payment processor logos
- **Typical lift:** 5-15%

#### Experiment: Money-Back Guarantee Badge
- **Hypothesis:** Visible guarantee reduces purchase anxiety.
- **Placement:** Near CTA and pricing
- **Typical lift:** 5-20% on purchase pages

#### Experiment: Add Live Chat / Support Indicator
- **Hypothesis:** Showing support availability reduces anxiety about post-purchase issues.
- **Format:** "Questions? Chat with us" or "Support responds in <2 hours"
- **Typical lift:** 5-10%

### Content & Messaging

#### Experiment: Video vs Text Explainer
- **Hypothesis:** A short explainer video on the landing page will increase engagement and conversions.
- **Control:** Text-only explanation
- **Variant:** 60-90 second video + text summary
- **Metric:** Time on page + conversion rate
- **Note:** Auto-play OFF. Provide play button.

#### Experiment: Objection Handling in FAQ
- **Hypothesis:** Adding an FAQ section that addresses top 5 objections will reduce bounce rate.
- **Research:** Survey non-converters or analyze support tickets for common objections
- **Typical lift:** 5-15%

## Experiment Execution Checklist

### Before Running
- [ ] Clear hypothesis written (If [change], then [metric] will [improve] because [reason])
- [ ] Single variable being tested (not multiple changes at once)
- [ ] Primary metric defined
- [ ] Sample size calculated (use online calculator for statistical significance)
- [ ] Test duration estimated (minimum 2 business weeks, avoid holidays)
- [ ] Both variants technically validated (no broken elements)

### During Test
- [ ] Don't peek too early (wait for full sample size)
- [ ] Monitor for technical issues (error rates, page speed)
- [ ] Don't make other changes to the page during the test
- [ ] Document any external factors (promotions, traffic spikes)

### After Test
- [ ] Statistical significance reached (95% confidence minimum)
- [ ] Practical significance assessed (>5% lift worth implementing?)
- [ ] Segment analysis done (does it work across all traffic sources?)
- [ ] Document results in experiment log
- [ ] Implement winner OR plan follow-up test
- [ ] Share learnings with team

## Experiment Prioritization (PIE Framework)

Score each experiment 1-10 on:

| Factor | Question |
|--------|----------|
| **Potential** | How much improvement could this test produce? |
| **Importance** | How valuable is the traffic on this page? |
| **Ease** | How easy is this to implement and test? |

**Priority Score** = (Potential + Importance + Ease) / 3

Run highest-scoring experiments first.
