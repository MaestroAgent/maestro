# Sales Methodology Reference

## Pipeline Stages & Exit Criteria

| Stage | Definition | Exit Criteria | Typical Duration |
|-------|-----------|---------------|-----------------|
| Lead | Initial contact, unqualified | Basic info collected, source identified | 1-3 days |
| Qualified | BANT confirmed, ICP fit verified | Budget/authority/need/timeline validated | 3-7 days |
| Proposal | Solution presented to stakeholders | Proposal delivered, decision-makers identified | 5-14 days |
| Negotiation | Terms being discussed | Commercial terms agreed, legal review complete | 7-21 days |
| Closed Won | Deal signed | Contract executed, handoff to onboarding | - |
| Closed Lost | Deal lost | Loss reason documented, lessons captured | - |

## MEDDIC Qualification Framework

| Element | Definition | Key Questions | Score 1-5 |
|---------|-----------|--------------|-----------|
| **Metrics** | Quantifiable business impact | What improvement do they expect? What's the cost of inaction? | 1=unknown, 5=quantified ROI |
| **Economic Buyer** | Person with budget authority | Who signs the check? Have you met them? | 1=unknown, 5=engaged champion |
| **Decision Criteria** | How they'll evaluate solutions | What are their must-haves vs nice-to-haves? | 1=unknown, 5=criteria match our strengths |
| **Decision Process** | Steps to get to a signed deal | Who's involved? What approvals needed? Timeline? | 1=unknown, 5=fully mapped with dates |
| **Identify Pain** | The business problem driving urgency | What triggered this initiative? What happens if they don't act? | 1=vague, 5=urgent quantified pain |
| **Champion** | Internal advocate pushing for you | Who's selling for you internally? Do they have influence? | 1=none, 5=active champion with access to EB |

**Scoring**: 25-30 = strong deal, 18-24 = moderate risk, 12-17 = high risk, <12 = unlikely to close

**Red flags**: No access to economic buyer. Champion can't articulate your value prop. Decision criteria favor a competitor. No compelling event or deadline.

## BANT Quick Qualification

### Budget
- "Is there budget allocated for this type of solution?"
- "What's the typical approval process for investments of this size?"
- "Have you purchased similar tools/services before? What did you spend?"

### Authority
- "Who else would be involved in this decision?"
- "Walk me through how a purchase like this typically gets approved."
- "Who would need to sign off?"

### Need
- "What's the biggest challenge you're trying to solve?"
- "What happens if you don't address this?"
- "How are you handling this today?"

### Timeline
- "When do you need this in place?"
- "What's driving that timeline?"
- "Are there any events or deadlines that create urgency?"

**Scoring**: Each dimension 1-5. Total 16-20 = hot, 11-15 = warm, 6-10 = nurture, <6 = disqualify.

## Objection Handling Patterns

Use the LAER framework: **Listen** (acknowledge), **Acknowledge** (validate), **Explore** (reframe), **Respond** (evidence + close).

| Category | Example Objection | Response Pattern |
|----------|------------------|-----------------|
| **Price** | "It's too expensive" | Acknowledge budget constraints. Reframe to ROI: "What's the cost of the problem you described?" Quantify value vs cost. Offer phasing if needed. |
| **Timing** | "Not right now" | Acknowledge. Explore the trigger: "What would need to change?" Identify the cost of delay. Propose a low-commitment next step. |
| **Competition** | "We're looking at X too" | Don't trash them. Ask "What criteria matter most?" Map your strengths to their criteria. Offer a side-by-side comparison on their terms. |
| **Authority** | "I need to check with..." | Help them sell internally: "What would they need to see?" Offer to join that conversation. Provide materials they can share. |
| **Status Quo** | "What we have works fine" | Quantify the gap: "You mentioned [pain]. What's that costing?" Paint the future state. Use peer examples: "Companies like yours typically see..." |

## Sales Metrics Benchmarks

| Metric | Strong | Average | Needs Work |
|--------|--------|---------|------------|
| Win rate | >30% | 15-30% | <15% |
| Avg sales cycle | <30 days | 30-60 days | >60 days |
| Pipeline coverage | >3x target | 2-3x target | <2x target |
| Lead → Qualified | >40% | 20-40% | <20% |
| Qualified → Proposal | >60% | 40-60% | <40% |
| Proposal → Won | >40% | 25-40% | <25% |
| Activity per deal | >10 touches | 5-10 touches | <5 touches |

**Key formulas**:
- Deal velocity = (# deals x avg value x win rate) / avg cycle days
- Pipeline gap = revenue target x (1 / win rate) - current pipeline
- Required leads = pipeline gap / avg deal size / lead-to-close rate

## Email Templates

### Cold Outreach (Pain-Based)
Subject: [Specific pain point] at [Company]

Hi [First Name],

[One-sentence observation about their company/role that shows research].

[Company] helps [target persona] solve [pain point] by [value prop]. [One sentence of proof: metric, customer name, or result].

Worth a 15-minute conversation to see if this applies to [Company]?

### Follow-Up After Meeting
Subject: Next steps from our conversation

Hi [First Name],

Thanks for the time today. Key takeaways:
1. [Pain point they confirmed]
2. [Solution approach discussed]
3. [Next step agreed]

I'll [specific action you committed to] by [date]. On your end, you mentioned [their action item].

### Proposal Cover
Subject: [Company] x [Your Company] — proposal

Hi [First Name],

Attached is the proposal we discussed. Summary:
- **Problem**: [their pain in their words]
- **Solution**: [your approach]
- **Investment**: [price]
- **Timeline**: [implementation plan]

I'd suggest we walk through this together — are you available [two specific times]?

### Breakup Email
Subject: Should I close this out?

Hi [First Name],

I've reached out a few times and haven't heard back. I completely understand if the timing isn't right.

I'll close out my notes on this for now. If [pain point] becomes a priority again, feel free to reach out anytime.
